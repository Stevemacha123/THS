History of Thika High School

Thika High school is a boys boarding school located in Thika town in Kiambu county. The scool was founded in 1951 while Kenya was still a British colony. It was founded by the alliance of protestant churches and then the church of Scotland mission (commonly known as the presbyterian church of East Africa or PCEA). It was originally set as the site of the current Thogoto Teachers College but due to its close proximity to Alliance boys high school, (another PCEA boys school) it was moved to Thika. Thika High school has been known for its academic and sports excellence since it's foundation. Today, Thika High school has over 1,500 students and around 200 teaching and non-teaching staff. The current principal is Mr. Julius Muraya and Mr. Theuri is the current deputy principal. Thika High school over the years has experienced a gradual growth in both student and staff numbers and also a growth in the institution's infrastructure.
